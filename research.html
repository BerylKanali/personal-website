Nurturing Responsible Student Tech Innovators Through Out-of-Classroom Upskilling in Kenya

This conference paper presents a replicable model for embedding pathways from Responsible Computing to Responsible Innovation in African computing education contexts through out-of-classroom upskilling and project work.

Authors: Chao Mbogho, Beryl Kanali, and Mark Tanui (Proceedings of the ACM Global Computing Education Conference 2025, Vol 1).

Preparing Computing Students for Real-World Data Challenges: Industry-Embedded Data Science Training in Africa

This case study examines an eight-month collaboration between nLine and KamiLimu where students used open power-quality datasets to build practical skills in data-driven problem solving, decision-making under uncertainty, and communication with non-technical stakeholders.

Authors: Beryl Kanali and collaborators from nLine and KamiLimu.
