Nurturing Responsible Student Tech Innovators Through Out‑of‑Classroom Upskilling in Kenya

This conference paper presents a replicable model for embedding pathways from Responsible Computing to Responsible Innovation (RC‑to‑RI) into computing education in African contexts. The authors show that out‑of‑classroom upskilling can instill transferable Responsible Innovation skills through project‑based work.

Authors: Chao Mbogho, Beryl Kanali, and Mark Tanui (Proceedings of the ACM Global Computing Education Conference 2025 Vol 1).

Preparing Computing Students for Real‑World Data Challenges: Industry‑Embedded Data Science Training in Africa

This case study explores an eight‑month collaboration between nLine, a data company that collects high‑resolution power reliability data across sub‑Saharan Africa, and KamiLimu, an award‑winning Kenyan mentorship programme for undergraduate Computer Science students. Through structured mentorship and projects using open power‑quality datasets, 11 students developed practical skills in problem formulation, decision‑making under uncertainty and communicating insights to non‑technical stakeholders. The study identifies gaps between academic preparation and real‑world practice and proposes design principles for academia‑industry partnerships that improve data literacy, workforce readiness and responsible data practice in Africa.

Authors: Beryl Kanali and collaborators from nLine and KamiLimu
