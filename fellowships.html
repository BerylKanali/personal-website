UbuntuNet Alliance Women Hackathon 2023 – Team Kenya (NovaNex)

After a rigorous selection process from over 300 applicants, this women‑only hackathon brought together 15 developers to Kampala, Uganda from 23‑25 October 2023. The participants were divided into five teams, each tasked with building innovative solutions for research and education. Team Kenya — Jane Jackline Waithira, Rose Caleno and Beryl Kanali — developed NovaNex, a collaborative learning and knowledge‑exchange platform. Other teams included Malawi (Research Aggregator), Zambia (CollabScholar), Uganda (Digitalizing the Education System) and Zimbabwe (KUSHA).

KamiLimu Mentorship Program – Cohort 6 Fellow (2022) & Committee Member (2022–2023)

I joined KamiLimu as a fellow in 2022 as part of Cohort 6 (2021) and later served on the program’s committee in 2022–2023. KamiLimu is an 8‑month structured mentorship program for Computer Science students in Kenya that complements classroom learning through one‑to‑one mentorship. The program helps students develop professional and personal skills, improve scholarship awareness and engage with industry partners. Since its inception, KamiLimu has mentored over 170 students from 19 universities with a balanced gender representation and pillars such as Community Engagement, Scholarship Awareness, Professional Development, Personal Development, and Innovation & ICT skills.
